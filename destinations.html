Gilgit-Baltistan

Hunza

Summary: Known for its beautiful valleys and Baltit Fort.

Attractions: Altit Fort, Attabad Lake, Hopper Glacier

Best Months: April to October

Culture/Food: Walnut cake, local dried fruits, Hunza apricots

Cost Range: PKR 12,000 – 20,000 (3-day trip)

Skardu

Summary: A gateway to K2 and base for mountain treks.

Attractions: Shangrila Lake, Shigar Fort, Deosai Plains

Best Months: May to September

Culture/Food: Balti cuisine, local yak meat dishes

Cost Range: PKR 15,000 – 25,000 (4-day trip)

Fairy Meadows

Summary: High-altitude grassland near Nanga Parbat.

Attractions: Trekking, camping, Nanga Parbat Base Camp

Best Months: June to September

Culture/Food: Traditional mountain dishes

Cost Range: PKR 10,000 – 18,000 (2-day trip)

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK)

Swat

Summary: Often called the “Switzerland of Pakistan.”

Attractions: Fizagat Park, Malam Jabba ski resort

Best Months: March to November

Culture/Food: Chapli kabab, trout fish

Cost Range: PKR 8,000 – 15,000 (3-day trip)

Kalam & Malam Jabba

Summary: Popular hill stations and ski resort.

Attractions: Skiing, chair lifts, lush forests

Best Months: December to February (for snow)

Culture/Food: Pashto cuisine, snow festivals

Cost Range: PKR 10,000 – 17,000 (2-day trip)

Naran & Kaghan

Summary: Lush green valleys and lakes.

Attractions: Saif-ul-Malook, Lulusar Lake, Babusar Top

Best Months: May to September

Culture/Food: Trout, makai roti, local tea

Cost Range: PKR 12,000 – 22,000 (3-day trip)
